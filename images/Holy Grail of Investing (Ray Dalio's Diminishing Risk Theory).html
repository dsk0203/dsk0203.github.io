Theory:

The key to diversification is using more assets. The less correlated those assets, the better. While I wish I had come up with the title of this article, it is attributed to Ray Dalio, of Bridgewater Associates a successful hedge fund. He referred to diversification as the Holy Grail of Investing in his book, Principles. Traditional diversification involves adding more assets at relatively high correlations. Wiser diversification is adding uncorrelated and low-correlation assets. Adding one uncorrelated asset (two assets in the portfolio) can reduce risk by 29 percent. Utilizing six uncorrelated assets reduces risks by nearly 60 percent.
